Pouch Cell Battery Design for Electric Vehicles

This project focuses on the development of an advanced pouch cell battery system tailored for electric vehicle applications. Our objective was to create a high-energy-density, lightweight, and modular battery solution optimized for performance and safety.

Key Features & Achievements

  • Innovative Design: Engineered a modular pouch cell battery pack to enhance scalability and packaging efficiency within EV chassis.
  • Advanced Material Selection: Utilized high-performance materials including NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt) cathodes, graphite, and SiOx anodes, achieving a high discharge capacity of 18.6Ah per cell.
  • Thermal Management: Developed efficient cooling strategies to maintain optimal operating temperatures and prevent thermal runaway.
  • Lightweight Structure: Designed the battery enclosure with lightweight materials without compromising structural integrity and safety standards.
  • Performance Optimization: Conducted extensive simulations and validation tests to optimize energy density, charge/discharge cycles, and power output under real-world driving conditions.
  • Safety and Reliability: Integrated multiple safety features, including overcurrent protection, thermal sensors, and reinforced cell casings.

Technical Specifications

Cell Chemistry NMC + Graphite + SiOx
Discharge Capacity 18.6 Ah
Nominal Voltage 3.7V
Cycle Life 1000+ cycles
Operating Temperature -20°C to 60°C
Weight 600g per cell

Challenges and Solutions

Throughout the project, we encountered key challenges such as thermal runaway risks, structural integration complexities, and optimizing energy efficiency. These were addressed through rigorous simulations, improved thermal insulation techniques, and iterative design improvements based on real-world testing data.

Collaboration & Outcomes

This project was successfully executed in collaboration with cross-functional teams, including electrical engineers, thermal specialists, and automotive designers. The outcome was a highly efficient and safe pouch cell battery system, meeting industry standards and ready for potential commercialization in next-generation electric vehicles.

21700 Cylindrical Cell Battery Design

This project focuses on the development of a high-performance 21700 cylindrical cell battery system. Our objective was to design a reliable and scalable battery solution that is suitable for a range of applications, including energy storage systems, portable power solutions, and industrial use. The project aimed at achieving an optimal balance between energy density, safety, and long-term performance.

Key Features & Achievements

  • Advanced Modular Design: Engineered a scalable cylindrical cell battery system that is versatile for different energy storage requirements and applications.
  • High-Energy Density: Designed to achieve optimal energy storage with a nominal capacity of 5.0Ah per cell, perfect for long-lasting performance.
  • Thermal Management: Developed efficient thermal management systems to prevent overheating, ensuring the battery operates within a safe temperature range and enhances battery life.
  • Durability: Optimized the cell design to handle a large number of charge and discharge cycles (2000+ cycles) while maintaining excellent capacity retention.
  • Safety Features: Incorporated various protective mechanisms, such as overcharge protection, overcurrent sensors, and advanced cell casings, ensuring the safety and reliability of the battery under all conditions.
  • Compact Design: Maintained a compact and lightweight design while ensuring the battery structure meets industry standards for robustness and performance.

Technical Specifications

Cell Chemistry NMC + Graphite
Discharge Capacity 5000 mAh
Nominal Voltage 3.7V
Cycle Life 700+ cycles
Operating Temperature -10°C to 60°C
Weight 70g per cell

Challenges and Solutions

One of the major challenges faced during this project was achieving a high energy density while ensuring thermal stability. To address this, we utilized advanced thermal management techniques such as specialized cooling fins and phase change materials (PCMs). Additionally, the cell design was iterated to enhance its resistance to mechanical stress and ensure its longevity.

Collaboration & Outcomes

This project was successfully carried out in collaboration with a team of mechanical engineers, materials scientists, and battery specialists. The outcome was a cutting-edge 21700 cylindrical cell battery, ready for use in diverse industries such as energy storage, portable power generation, and more. The battery has undergone extensive testing and validation and is positioned to provide high reliability and performance for years to come.
